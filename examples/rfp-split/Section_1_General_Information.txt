SECTION 1: GENERAL INFORMATION
1.1 INTRODUCTION
The State of Oregon, acting by and through the Department of Administrative Services, Procurement
Services (“DAS PS”), on behalf of Department of Administrative Services’ Enterprise Information
Services (“EIS”) and all other Authorized Purchasers, including all Oregon Cooperative Procurement
Program (“ORCPP”) members, is issuing this Request for Proposal (“RFP”) to establish one or more
Price Agreements for the purchase of Cybersecurity Awareness Training Services and Materials.
The State desires the services of one or more information security education and awareness
contractors to provide materials and services to support user education awareness programs, which
must target numerous audiences, and which will be made available for purchasing by multiple state
agencies and local governments through one or more statewide price agreements. A Proposer does
not need to offer all the training materials and services listed in this RFP to be considered for
award.
DAS PS intends to enter into one or more Price Agreements for Cybersecurity Awareness Training
Services and Materials. Any purchases under a Price Agreement must be authorized by purchase
orders issued by an Authorized Purchaser, and purchases may only be made by ORCCP members in
accordance with the ordering procedures described in the Buyers’ Guide that will accompany the
issued Price Agreement(s). If more than one Price Agreement is awarded, selection and award of
contract(s) to be entered into under the Price Agreements will follow a Best Value process.
The initial term of the Price Agreement(s) is anticipated to be 5 years with options to renew up to a
cumulative maximum of 15 years for each Price Agreement.
The State will consider Proposals from Proposers that can provide the necessary goods and/or
services that fulfill the identified needs for cybersecurity awareness training services and materials.
Additional details on the scope of the Goods and Services are included in the Scope section.
DAS’ EIS contains within its organizational structure the office of Cyber Security Services (“CSS”). CSS
brings together enterprise security capabilities into a single organization. CSS is responsible under
ORS 276A.300 for enterprise security policy, security monitoring of the state network, enterprise
incident response, and enterprise security architecture, as well as dissemination of security training,
policy, and best practices across state government. CSS desires to engage in one or more contracts
for the Goods and Services, following a Best Value process in the event of award of more than one
Price Agreement, for cybersecurity awareness training services and materials suitable for its program
goals and needs.
Notwithstanding the foregoing, Proposers are advised that there is no guarantee of any specific
amount of purchases under a Price Agreement. Further, notwithstanding the award of a Price
Agreement, DAS PS may conduct separate solicitations or award project-specific contracts or
purchase orders for Goods or Services similar to what is described within this RFP using any method
allowed by law.
